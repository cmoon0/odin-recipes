Roasted Tomato Soup

The simplest soup possible that doesn't come from a tin. If you have extra tomatoes going off in the bottom of the fridge, add them too. You can also roast some bell peppers alongside the tomatoes for a little extra flavour. If you are pressed for time (or energy), roast the onions with the tomatoes instead of cooking them seperately. This makes it a little less flavourful but it is much easier.

Serves about 4 depending on how much extra liquid is added towards the end. This recipe is designed to be adaptable feel free to improvise with extra herbs and spices (try adding a little paprika or chilli powder when cooking the onions down).

Dont worry about fully caramalizeing the onions, they only need to be soft. If you want to delve into the world of caramelising onions, try this recipe.

Ingredients

  • 1kg tomatos
  • 3 small onions
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon tomato puree
  • salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Quater all the tomatos and add to a rimmed baking tray. Drizzle with oil and make sure they are coated. Season generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Roast in the Oven at 180°C for at least 20 minutes or until the skin peels.
  3. While the tomatos roast, slice all the onions and garlic and add to a pot on low heat. Drizzle in oil and begin to caramelise until the tomatos are done.
  4. Once the tomatoes are soft, pour into the pot with the onions, making sure to get all the juices from the baking tray.
  5. Stir together with the tomato puree and scrape up the bottom of the pot if any of the onions burnt on. If needed, add a few tablepoons of water
  6. Blitz with an immersion blender until smooth (to preference) and serve.
